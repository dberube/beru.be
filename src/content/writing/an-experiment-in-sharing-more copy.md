I’ve spent more than 20 years building products, leading teams, launching companies, and working through hard problems inside startups and some of the world’s largest organizations.

I haven’t shared much of it publicly.

Most of what I’ve learned has stayed inside meetings, product documents, conversations with teams, or my own head. I’ve usually been more interested in doing the work than talking about it.

So this site is an experiment in sharing more.

I wanted one place to bring together the products I’ve helped lead, the things I’m building myself, and the ideas that shape how I work. More than a portfolio, I wanted a home on the internet I could call my own, a place to write, share what I’m thinking, and let the work and ideas live together.

I’ll write about product leadership, innovation, AI, building quickly, and the craft and judgment required to turn ideas into products that create real value.

I’ll also share things I’m building myself. Products, tools, and agents that help me explore new ideas and stay close to how products actually get made.

I don’t have a publishing schedule or a fully formed plan for what this becomes. That’s part of the experiment.

The goal is to share what I’m learning, sharpen my thinking in public, and see where it leads.

This is the start.
